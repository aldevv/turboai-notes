## Section 1: Introduction (30 seconds)

Hey everyone. Today I'm going to show you TurboAI Notes — a clean, minimal note-taking app that I built entirely using Claude Code, Anthropic's AI coding agent.

The app was generated from a Figma design file using a structured three-phase process: planning, building the frontend and backend in parallel, and then polishing everything together.

Let's jump in.

---

## Section 2: Sign Up (1 minute)

When you first visit the app, you land on the login page. You can see a friendly cactus mascot and a clean form — just email and password.

I don't have an account yet, so let me click the link to sign up.

Here's the signup page. Notice the sleeping cat mascot and the greeting — "Yay, New Friend!" Small details like this give the app its personality.

I'll type in my email and password. I can click the eye icon to show or hide my password.

Now I'll hit Sign Up...

And just like that, I'm in. Behind the scenes, signing up automatically created three default categories for me — we'll see those in a moment.

---

## Section 3: The Main Dashboard (1 minute)

This is the notes dashboard. On the left is a sidebar with my three categories: Random Thoughts, School, and Personal — each with its own color.

In the center, I have a blank slate. The boba cup mascot tells me I haven't created any notes yet.

Let me click "+ New Note" up here in the top right.

An editor opens instantly in full-screen. The note was created on the server the moment I clicked. Now I can start typing.

I'll give it a title... and add some content...

Notice there's no save button. The app auto-saves as I type — you can see the "Last edited" timestamp updating in the top corner. It's debounced, so it saves a half-second after I stop typing.

Now let me assign this note to a category. I'll click the dropdown at the top and select School.

The note is updated immediately. I'll close the editor with the X button.

Back on the dashboard, my note appears as a card — with the title, a preview of the content, the date, and a colored badge for the School category.

---

## Section 4: Category Filtering (1 minute)

Let me create a couple more notes so we can see filtering in action.

I'll quickly make one for Random Thoughts... and one for Personal.

Now look at the sidebar — the note counts next to each category have updated automatically.

If I click on "School" in the sidebar, the grid filters to show only my School notes.

Click "Personal" — now I see only Personal notes.

And clicking "All Categories" brings everything back.

It's a simple but effective way to stay organized without a lot of complexity.

---

## Section 5: Editing and Deleting (45 seconds)

Let me open one of my notes and make a change.

I'll click the card to open it... update the title... and again, it auto-saves.

I can also reassign it to a different category right from the editor.

To delete a note, I'll close the editor and... delete the note from the card.

It's gone, and the category count updates to reflect that.

---

## Section 6: Sign Out (15 seconds)

Finally, signing out is simple. I click "Sign out" at the bottom of the sidebar.

I'm immediately returned to the login page, ready for the next user.

## Closing (30 seconds)

That's TurboAI Notes. Clean authentication, instant note creation, auto-save, category filtering — all built from scratch with a Django backend, a Next.js frontend, and a consistent, friendly design.

The entire app was generated by Claude Code from a Figma design. No boilerplate, no templates — just the design and the AI.
